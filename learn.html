Finance 101

Finance 101

What do these terms mean?

Net Income

At its basic, net income defines a company’s total earnings or profit. Simply put, net income is what you get when you subtract all expenses (including tax expenses) from revenue.


  • When a company’s net income increases, it’s normally a result of either revenue increasing or expenses being slashed.
  • It goes without saying that an increase in net income is generally perceived as positive and factors into a stock’s performance.

EBITDA

It stands for earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, and amortization and is calculated by subtracting operating expenses from revenue and adding back depreciation and amortization to operating profit (aka EBIT).


Does EBITDA paints a rawer image of profitability compared to net income. While some proponents of EBITDA claim that it’s a less-complicated look at a company’s financial health, critics state that it oversimplifies earnings and creates misleading measurements of company profitability.

EPS

Earnings per share (EPS) is one of the most common items highlighted in an earnings announcement, and it provides investors insight into a company’s earnings health, and often affects its stock price after an announcement.


EPS is calculated by taking net income, subtracting the preferred dividends (for the sake of simplicity, let’s assume Hemlock Incorporated doesn't pay dividends on preferred shares), and dividing that difference by the average number of outstanding shares.

Budgeting

A budget or spending plan is a road map for telling your money what to do each month. At its simplest, a budget lists how much income you have coming in compared to what's going out each month.


Benefits of creating a budget:

  • Make smarter decisions with your finances daily
  • See a clear picture of how much money you have
  • Stop and think before every purchase

Cutting Expenses

After you've created a budget, you'll have a better understanding of where your money goes and where to trim expenses. For many people, this means cutting back on some of the little things that can add up.


Why is reducing expenses important:

  • It can free up more money in your budget
  • Extra money helps you pay off debts faster
  • Boost your emergency fund or grow retirement savings

Insurance

When you've built a solid financial footing for you and your family, you need to protect it. Accidents and disasters can and do happen, and if you aren’t adequately insured, it could leave you in financial ruin.


You need insurance to:

  • Protect your life
  • Protect your ability to earn income
  • Keep a roof over your head
